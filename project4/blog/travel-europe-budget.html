Traveling Europe on $1000 a Month

Image credit: Shannon Paton All Rights Reserved

Want to know how you can travel in Europe on a low budget?

Seeing new sites can be cheap when sticking to a budget and taking advantage of free opportunities.
Image credit: Daniel van den Berg/Unsplash

There are many ways you can reduce the costs of your travels. I traveled on a budget of $1000 for two months in Europe. I chose Spain and Portugal since they were less expensive and had warm weather in the months of February and March. Here are a couple of tips that I followed to meet my budget:

1. Pick less expensive cities and countries

Research inexpensive countries and cities to visit. Some top affordable options include The Czech Republic, Bulgaria, Romania, Greece, and Ireland.

2. Stay in hostels for cheap

An affordable and safe option for traveling is hostels. There are youth hostels or hostels that accept people of all ages. Hostels are great option for making friends while solo traveling as well.

3. Volunteer or for free accommodations

Workaway or WWOOF are two resources for working in exchange for free accommodations. I had a great experience volunteering at a hostel through Workaway in Portugal with other volunteers from around the world.

4. Find free and cheap activities

While there are lots of opportunities to spend money on tourist attractions, you can limit spending on these and enjoy free or low-cost activities. Examples include siteseeing on the street, visiting parks and natural reserves, and buying food from the local market for a picnic. You can use the extra money in your budget for attractions that are at the top of your list.
