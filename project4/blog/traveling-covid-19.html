Traveling and COVID-19

Image credit: Lisanto 李奕良/Unsplash

Here are a few tips for traveling safely during COVID-19.

Social distancing is important according to local mandates and COVID rates in the area you are visiting. Image credit: Jon Tyson/Unsplash/Unsplash

Traveling has become much more complicated since the onset of COVID-19 and public health concerns. Please see the CDC guide for current guidance on COVID-19.

1. Research the country you are visiting and current COVID-19 precautions.

The country or city you are visiting may have more strict guidelines than where you currently live. Check local mandates and requirements before traveling.

2. Make sure you meet vaccine and testing requirements for traveling.

Additionally, depending on where you’re planning to travel, there may be requirements to have a negative test regardless of vaccination status before getting on the plane. Make sure to book appointments with plenty of time in advance so that you are approved for your travel.

3. Consider not traveling to certain locations.

If COVID rates are especially high in the destination you are considering going, it might be time to choose a different location or cancel your trip. Consider the impact on the local community if they are experiencing resource shortages. Getting travel insurance is a good idea in case you need to cancel your trip for any reason.
